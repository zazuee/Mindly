Stories

Read Other Stories From People That Experienced Mental Health Issues

Barry Du Bois, an Australian TV presenter, faced numerous personal tragedies, including the loss of his mother to cancer, multiple failed IVF attempts, and his wife's cancer battle. These experiences led him into a deep depression. In 2017, he was diagnosed with incurable Multiple Myeloma and given just three months to live. Instead of giving up, Barry embraced vulnerability, sought support from his wife, and focused on mindfulness and community connection. He now advocates for mental health awareness, emphasizing that open conversations and emotional honesty are strengths, especially for men.
Actor Taye Diggs shared his journey of understanding and supporting his sister Christian, who was diagnosed with schizophrenia in her twenties. Initially, Taye distanced himself due to fear and misunderstanding. Over time, he educated himself about the condition and became a steadfast supporter of his sister. Together, they launched the "Live Your PosSCZible" campaign to raise awareness about schizophrenia and challenge societal misconceptions. Their story highlights the importance of compassion, education, and sustained connection for those navigating mental health challenges.
Jazz Thornton, a New Zealand mental health activist, survived multiple suicide attempts during her teenage years. Drawing from her experiences, she co-founded the suicide prevention organization "Voices of Hope" to advocate for mental health awareness. Jazz has authored books, directed documentaries like "Jessica's Tree," and produced viral videos to share stories of hope and recovery. Her work has been recognized globally, and she continues to inspire others by promoting open conversations about mental health and the importance of seeking help.
